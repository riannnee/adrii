INTRODUCTION

A motherboard (sometimes known as the Mainboard, system board, and logic board) is the main printed circuit board (PCB) in a computer system. This is where many of the crucial components are located, such as the CPU and memory. It is considered as the backbone of the computer as it allows communication between all internal components such as the memory, processor, graphics card, CPU, storage devices and others.


DISCUSSION

Form Factor refers to the size, shapes, dimensions and layouts of a desktop computer and determines how the board fits in the computer case. The build is the overall design and structure of the motherboard, while CPU slots are where the CPU is located. Memory slot refers to the number of slots (memory) that the system can hold. Chipsets are a set of electronic components that manage data transfer between I/O devices and are often called the brain of the motherboard. The BIOS or basic input/output system lets users access and set up the system and includes instructions on how to load basic computer hardware (UEFI is a modern version). PCI or Peripheral Component Interconnect Slots are expansions where extra components like graphic cards can be added. SATA or Serial Advanced Technology Attachment is used to connect hard drives and solid-state drives to the motherboard.

There are many different types of motherboards to meet different computing needs. Large motherboards such as ATX, Standard-ATX, and Extended ATX offer many CPU and memory slots and advanced chipsets. Airflow and cooling condition of these motherboards also maintains a smooth performance despite the heavy workload. This makes them ideal for gaming and professional setups that require high-end graphics and overclocking. On the other hand, smaller motherboards such as Micro-ATX, Mini-ATX, and Mini-ITX give emphasis on space efficiency and cost over expansion. Compared to the previous boards, these types support fewer PCI slots but have compact builds to compensate for the limited space. AT and LPX motherboards have old designs that have been replaced as newer motherboards have been developed. These motherboard designs have limited designs and compatibility. Lastly, the BTX and Pico-BTX designs provide more efficient airflow and cooling but are not widely adopted.

In conclusion, the various types of motherboard designs show the development of technology in recent years and how it has adapted to the needs of the users and demands for high-performance computing systems. Nowadays, users have a wide range of options depending on their needs and preferences.
