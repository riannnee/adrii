COMPUTER SCIENCE AS A DISCIPLINE

Computer Science is the academic discipline concerned with computing. The intellectual processes of the discipline combine theory, abstraction, and design. Problems are solved in a distinctive manner, with an overall goal of identifying and applying recurring conceptual principles. Although these intellectual processes derive from other disciplines, a computer scientist’s perspective offers a unique and important voice in a community of learning such as a liberal-arts college.

Computer science became a discipline in the late 1950s and early 1960s. The field grew out of the efforts of electrical engineers and mathematicians to build devices capable of automated mechanical computation. Computer scientists study how to build the physical devices that comprise computing systems, how to create the algorithms and software systems that control these devices, and the more theoretical questions pertaining to what problems even have algorithmic solutions.

► COMPUTER ENGINEERING

Computer engineering (CE) students study the design of digital hardware and software systems including communications systems, computers and devices that contain computers. Computer engineering integrates principles from electrical engineering and computer science to design and develop hardware and software systems for computing applications, with a broad understanding of computing technologies and their integration with hardware components.

Key Subjects and Skills:

• Embedded systems

• Digital systems design

• Computer architecture

Career Paths:

• Hardware engineer

• Embedded systems engineer

• Network engineer

• Systems architect

► COMPUTER SCIENCE

Computer science (CS) spans the range from theory through programming to cutting-edge development of computing solutions. Computer science offers a foundation that permits graduates to adapt to new technologies and new ideas. Computer scientists often work on the design and development of software and systems, and their expertise is crucial in advancing the field of computing. They play a significant role in various industries, including entertainment

Key Subjects and Skills:

• Programming

• Algorithms

• Data structures

Career Paths:

• Software development

• Web development and design

• Cybersecurity


► SOFTWARE ENGINERRING

Software engineering (SE) focuses on practical applications, processes, and techniques for building software systems. It involves the design, development, testing, and maintenance of software systems, applying engineering principles and methodologies, to meet specific requirements.

Key Subjects and Skills:

• Software design and development

• Project management

Career Paths:

• Software development

• Systems analysis

• DevOps engineering

► DATA SCIENCE

Data science is a multidisciplinary field that involves extracting insights and knowledge from structured and unstructured data using various techniques, including statistics, machine learning, and computer science.

Key Subjects and Skills:

• Data analysis

• Statistics and probability

• Machine learning

Career Paths:

• Data analytics

• Machine learning

► INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY

Information Technology (IT) primarily deals with the implementation, maintenance, and use of computer systems, networks, and software to manage and process data. It involves tasks such as installing and configuring hardware and software, managing databases, troubleshooting technical issues, and providing technical support to users. IT professionals often work on the practical application of technology to meet the needs of organisations and individuals. They assume responsibility for selecting hardware and software products appropriate for an organization.

Key Subjects and Skills:

• Network management

• Software development

• Database management

Career Paths:

• Software development

• Database administration

• IT consultant




ANALYSIS / REACTION

Computer Science (CS) emerged as a distinct discipline in the late 1950s and early 1960s. Today, it remains one of the most significant and rapidly evolving fields of study in today’s fast-paced world. Computer Science is an academic discipline that revolves around the study of computers, developing theoretical foundations, design, and practical applications. It equips students and professionals with the necessary knowledge and skills to understand technology on a deeper level and how to utilize it to address problems prevalent in our society. Computer Science is now used in almost every industry, including healthcare, education, business, agriculture, and entertainment.

Some researchers argue that computer science is more closely related to mathematics than to science itself. In an article written by Black (2024), he argued whether computer science covers an area of scientific examination. He noted that calling Computer Science ‘science’ is a fraud because humans have created computers when physics, biology, and chemistry already exist in the world. However, I disagree with this perspective. Science is both a body of knowledge and the process for building that knowledge. It is a means to evaluate and create new knowledge. Computer scientists formulate hypotheses, use experimentation, develop theoretical models, and validate results in a manner similar to others in other scientific disciplines. But in contrast to traditional science, computer science is primarily concerned with the study of artificial systems.

I came to realize that studying a computing discipline is broader than I thought. It extends far beyond the area of programming and offers a wide range of career options. Computing is a family of various disciplines that branches into several more disciplines. These include Computer Engineering (CE), which aims to develop and design software and hardware systems; Computer Science (CS), which focuses on the theory of computational systems and practical aspects such as operating systems, algorithms, and programming ; Software Engineering (SE), which deals with the design, development and maintenance of software applications; Data Science (DS), which focus on data analysis and statistics; and Information Technology (IT), which manage computer systems and networks. A student pursuing a degree in these disciplines will be able to specialize in areas such as cybersecurity, robotics, web development, networking, business analytics, and many more.

It amazes me how Computer Science provides students with numerous opportunities for careers related to technology and how this field is beneficial in other disciplines, such as medicine. This academic discipline enables individuals to be creative while employing logic and innovation to devise solutions that address real-world problems. As technology continues to evolve, its influence on society becomes more evident, and new areas of specialization continue to emerge. I know this will be a course that I will not regret pursuing
